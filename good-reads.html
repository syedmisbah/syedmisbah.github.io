Good Reads

There's a lot of knowledge and content everywhere, but it's hard to find quality stuff. This is an eclectic collection of things I read, watched, and was moved or educated by. A constant work in progress.

Stimulating Reads

  • Hacker News

    The one stop source for all the latest news in technology. Far eclipses competitors like TechCrunch in quality and speed.

  • Paul Graham's Essays

    Founder of Y Combinator writes simple yet captivating essays about startups, knowledge, and how to live life.

  • Andrej Karpathy's Blog

    Smart hacker and researcher doing amazing things with neural nets. His intuitive guide to neural nets is the best I've seen.

  • Sam Altman's Blog

    Speaks about startups, technology and entrepreneurship.

  • FiveThirtyEight

    Nate Silver's data-centric blog. Gained popularity predicting the 2008 US elections and continues to publish interesting data-driven reads.

  • Farnam Street

    Helps you go to bed smarter than you woke up. Philosophy, wisdom, happiness, and meaning.

  • Essays and Speeches by Charlie Munger

    Long-time friend and advisor to Warren Buffett on economics, finance, psychology, and worldly wisdom.

  • Noam Chomsky's Articles

    Possibly the world's leading intellectual. His thoughts on media, government, policy and linguistics are a formative influence.

  • Schneier on Security

    Bruce Schneier is the Chuck Norris of computer security. Former NSA official, long-time code breaker, read by a quarter million people.

  • Krebs on Security

    Former Washington Post reporter writing accessibly about current security exploits and hacks.

  • Philip Guo's Blog

    Author of The PhD Grind. Great advice for students, programmers and more.

  • Moxie Marlinspike's Hacks

    Software by the legendary hacker Moxie, particularly network security tools.

News and Magazines

  • The New York Times

    One of the best Opinion pages out there.

  • The New Yorker

    Covers politics, pop culture, and the cultural life of New York City.

  • The Atlantic

    Particularly good reads on education and long-form journalism.

  • The Wall Street Journal

    Top of the line source for business and economic news.

Movies and Documentaries

  • How the Economic Machine Works - Ray Dalio

    Founder of Bridgewater Associates explains how the economy works in 30 animated minutes. A must-watch for anyone who uses money.

  • Inside Job

    A documentary explaining the corruption that caused the 2008 economic recession.

  • Midas Formula: The Trillion Dollar Bet

    The story of Long Term Capital Management - a Nobel Laureate hedge fund that made a fortune and then collapsed.

  • Enron: The Smartest Guys in the Room

    How one of the biggest business scandals in history happened.

  • The Internet's Own Boy: The Story of Aaron Swartz

    A riveting story of injustice served on a brave-heart using computer science to make political change.

  • Pirates of Silicon Valley

    The original Silicon Valley movie (1999) — Steve Jobs, Wozniak, Bill Gates, and the founding of an era.

  • Randy Pausch: The Last Lecture

    One of the most inspirational videos I've ever seen. A cancer-diagnosed CS professor at CMU gives the ultimate lecture on life's lessons.

  • Rush

    The epic F1 rivalry between Niki Lauda and James Hunt. No clear antagonist or protagonist.

  • City of God

    Semi-fictional Portuguese movie following crime in Brazil. Featured 2nd on Roger Ebert's best of 2003.

  • Fire in Babylon

    The best cricket documentary I've seen. West Indies in the 70s and 80s — undefeated for 15 years, fighting apartheid.

  • Food Inc

    Exposes how the US food industry optimizes profits by forgoing healthy food production.

  • Inside the Dark Web

    Surveillance, privacy, Tor, Silk Road, bitcoin, and the deep web.

  • The Pirate Bay: Away From Keyboard

    Three computer guys, a torrent site, and a battle with the music and film industry.

  • The Social Network

    How Facebook was founded, and the legal battle that followed.

  • An Inconvenient Truth

    The reality of climate change and global warming.

  • Gandhi

    Gandhi's journey and struggle for Indian independence.

  • The Square

    The Egyptian Revolution of 2011 in Cairo's Tahrir Square.

  • The Reluctant Fundamentalist

    Based on Mohsin Hamid's novel. A powerful movie with a strong message.

Academic Papers

  • Deep Visual-Semantic Alignments for Generating Image Descriptions

    Karpathy and Fei-Fei Li [2014] - Stanford generates English sentences describing images using CNNs and RNNs.

  • ImageNet Classification with Deep Convolutional Neural Networks

    Krizhevsky, Sutskever, Hinton [2012] - 60M parameter CNN, error rate 15.3% vs previous record of 26.2%.

  • DeepFace: Closing the Gap to Human-Level Performance in Face Verification

    Taigman, Yang et al [2014] - Facebook AI. 120M parameter, 9-layer deep net. 97.35% accuracy on 4M images.

  • Intriguing Properties of Neural Networks

    Szegedy, Zaremba et al [2014] - Shows adversarial examples: visually identical images that fool neural nets.

  • The Visual Microphone: Passive Recovery of Sound from Video

    MIT CSAIL [2014] - Recovering sound from minute vibrations in silent video footage. Straight out of sci-fi.

  • W32.Stuxnet Dossier

    Falliere et al [2011] - The infamous Stuxnet worm that used zero-day exploits to wipe out 1/5th of Iran's nuclear centrifuges.

  • Security Analysis of India's Electronic Voting Machines

    Wolchok et al [2010] - Demonstrated two attacks that could undermine the world's largest democracy with brief physical access.

  • Internet Censorship in Iran

    Aryan and Halderman [2013] - Network measurements from a major Iranian ISP during the 2013 presidential election.

  • Internet Censorship in China

    Xu, Mao, Halderman et al [2011] - Mapping where the IDS devices of the Great Firewall of China are placed.

  • Spying the World from your Laptop

    Le Blond et al [2010] - From a single machine, collected 148M IPs downloading 2B copies of torrents over 103 days.

  • Crawling BitTorrent DHTs for Fun and Profit

    Wolchok and Halderman [2010] - Tracking 7.9M IPs downloading 1.5M torrents over 16 days.

  • Romantic Partnerships and the Dispersion of Social Ties

    Backstrom and Kleinberg [2013] - Predicting your romantic interest from Facebook data with 79% accuracy.

  • First Person Hyperlapse Videos

    Kopf, Cohen et al [2014] - Converting shaky GoPro footage into smooth hyperlapse videos.

  • What's the Big Idea? Towards a Pedagogy of Idea Power

    Papert [2000] - How schools deform ideas to fit the pedagogical framework, and the role of CS in fixing that.

Television Series

  • Breaking Bad

    A chemistry teacher starts making meth to fund his cancer treatment. The character development is heart-wrenching.

  • House of Cards

    Political drama showing just how dirty politics can get.

  • Sherlock

    Modern take on the classical detective story with Benedict Cumberbatch. Three long episodes a season — a quick awesome watch.

Humor

  • xkcd

    Randall Munroe's comic strip about technology, science, mathematics and relationships. Read by every technologist.

  • SMBC

    Saturday Morning Breakfast Cereal — atheism, romance, science, and the meaning of life.

  • PhD Comics

    Follows graduate students' lives and stories. Written by Caltech instructor Jorge Cham.

  • The Oatmeal

    Famous for its Nikola Tesla and Mantis Shrimp comics. Inman says it takes 7-8 hours of research per comic.

  • Reddit

    The internet's front page. Daily dose of humor, news, and rabbit holes.
